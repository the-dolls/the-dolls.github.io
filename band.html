About The Dolls

Formed in early 2010, The Dolls began as an eight-girl lineup and quickly built a devoted following. Two members left the band in 2024, after which the band paused its forward momentum and entered a long period of reduced activity. The group reunited in 2025 and resumed performing together. Velvet Doll

Lead Vocals + Lead Guitar

Velvet Doll commands the stage with a powerful voice and razor-sharp guitar work. Her songwriting blends intimate vulnerability with anthemic hooks, giving the band's songs both emotional weight and arena-ready power. Onstage she moves effortlessly between whispered verses and soaring choruses, leaving audiences both moved and electrified. Her greatest skill is surviving a show without dropping the mic.

Sporty Doll

Bass Guitar

Sporty Doll anchors the band with her solid bass lines and infectious energy. She can effortlessly play the bass with her eyes closed while doing squats on one foot. Her rhythmic precision and groove-oriented style provide the perfect foundation for the band's dynamic sound. Offstage, she's known for her athleticism and competitive spirit, often challenging bandmates to impromptu fitness contests backstage.

Crazy Doll

Drums

Crazy Doll was found while banging on pots and pans under a bridge. Her hit blew up, gaining milions of fans (the mice that lived there also). The band loves her, but her neighbours keep their distane. Her skills are unmatched, nobody else being able to make rats rock fans. One time she tried to play using the heads of the members, giving Dreamy Doll a concussion.

Dreamy Doll

Flautist

Dreamy Doll adds flute melodies into the band's arrangements, blending classical technique with ambient textures. Her lyrical phrasing and tasteful use of effects create haunting interludes that lift songs into cinematic space. She thought it was an audition for a symphony orchestra but stayed anyway because the lights were cool. The shounds of her flute make cats dance and flowers bloom. One time she missed rehearsal because her flute neaded a free day.
